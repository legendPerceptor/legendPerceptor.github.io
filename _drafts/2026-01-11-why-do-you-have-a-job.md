Here is a simplified definition of a job: **one or several ways to help your employer get richer**. In certain fields, loyalty, the spirit of sacrifices, and faithfulness may be invaluable, but in a regular business world, the sentence above is what a job means. Tons of workers never thought about the reason behind getting a job. They tend to think of it as a way to make a living for themselves so they struggle or try their best to keep their position by working over time or doing something that they think will please their leaders. Working hard has certain emotional value to show your leader that you are a reliable person. But at the end, a rational business owner or leadership team always cares more about spreadsheets and revenues, which means the result of your contribution weighs much higher than the amount of time you put in. A famous YouTuber Ali Abdaal recently shared a video titled [How to Get Rich on Easy Mode](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PKYfRofUdg)，where he clarified the fundamental logic behind getting rich and elaborated why helping another business get richer is the easiest way to get you rich.

In this article, I mainly want to address the following three aspects: (1) Why you have a job and how to utilize the job to fulfil both your personal goals and your employer's goals; (2) Why competition is so meaningless and how does `altruism` help yourself in the long run; (3) Can you work for yourself instead of working for an employer and how should we make a rational decision on this.

When talking about having a job, you have to think of it from the employer's perspective.
